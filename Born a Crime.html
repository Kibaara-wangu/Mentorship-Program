Born a Crime

Chapter 1 & 2 Summary

The book "Born A Crime" by Trevor Noah is a story about a mixed-race child
growing up during apartheid in South Africa. In two chapters, the author narrates
his experiences of being raised by a strict Christian mother in a racially segregated society.

In Chapter 1, titled "Run," Trevor shares the challenges he and his mother faced due
to apartheid's racial segregation. The chapter highlights the tribal conflicts between the
Zulu and Xhosa tribes and depicts Trevor's struggle to come to terms with his mother's
unwavering faith. Despite his reluctance to attend church every Sunday, Trevor's mother was
determined to keep him safe, going so far as to throw him out of a moving car to protect him from a gang attack.

In chapter 2, titled "Born A Crime," explores apartheid's oppressive laws. Trevor's existence,
as the child of a white father and a black mother, was considered a crime under these laws. The chapter
sheds light on the cruelty of apartheid and its impact on Trevor's life.

Drawing parallels with my hometown Nyeri, it is evident that the theme of resilience in the face of
misfortune is universal. In Nyeri, we grapple with issues of water scarcity, climate change, and land
disputes, which mirror the systemic injustices Trevor describes in his book. In addition, there are disparities
in church relations, with some churches catering to the rich while others serve the average population.
