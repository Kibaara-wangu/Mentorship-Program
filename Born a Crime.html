Chapter 1 & 2 Summary

This text delves into the first two chapters of "Born A Crime," a book by Trevor Noah. These chapters provide a detailed account of the
life of a mixed-race child growing up during apartheid in South Africa, raised by a strict Christian mother.

"Run," Trevor narrates the story of his mother's unwavering faith and the challenges they faced due to the racial segregation imposed by apartheid.
This chapter highlights the tribal conflicts between the Zulu and Xhosa tribes, and how these conflicts impacted the lives of people living in South Africa at the time. Despite being raised in a Christian household, Trevor is not fully convinced that they should go to church every Sunday. However, his mother is governed by her faith and wouldn't accept any of Trevor's excuses to skip church. This chapter also depicts Trevor's
mother's determination to keep her son safe, even if it means throwing him out of a moving car to save him from a gang attack.
